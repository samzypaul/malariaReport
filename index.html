World Malaria Report 2025

Addressing the Threat of Antimalarial Drug Resistance & Tracking Global Progress

The Global Burden

Despite decades of global health interventions, malaria remains a formidable adversary. In 2023, the disease continued to impact millions across 85 endemic countries. While the peak surges observed during the height of the COVID-19 pandemic have stabilized, the current trajectory is off-track to meet the 2030 Global Technical Strategy targets. The staggering sheer volume of cases and deaths requires immediate, sustained international action.

Disproportionate Impact

Malaria does not affect the world equally. The WHO African Region bears a vastly disproportionate share of the global burden. The disease ravages populations in sub-Saharan Africa, with the highest concentration of fatalities occurring among the most vulnerable demographic: young children whose immune systems have not yet developed partial resistance to the Plasmodium parasite.

Global Case Distribution

The WHO African Region accounts for an overwhelming 94% of all global malaria cases, highlighting a massive regional health disparity.

Mortality Demographics in Africa

Children under the age of 5 represent 76% of all malaria-related deaths within the African Region, marking them as the highest-risk group.

Mortality: Pandemic Setbacks

To understand the current crisis, it is crucial to look at recent historical data. Before the COVID-19 pandemic severely disrupted supply chains and healthcare access, global malaria mortality was slowly trending downwards. While the current 2023 figures show a recovery from peak pandemic mortality, we are still losing more lives to malaria today than we were in 2019.

Although down from pandemic peaks, the 2023 mortality estimate of 608,000 remains significantly higher than the 588,000 deaths recorded in 2019, underscoring the urgent need to regain momentum.

Glimmers of Hope & Progress

Amidst the daunting statistics, there are significant public health victories. Concerted national efforts have led to complete elimination in certain regions, and the deployment of new biological tools, such as advanced vaccines, is reshaping the landscape of preventative care in high-burden nations.

Critical Challenges & Emerging Risks

The path to the 2030 Global Technical Strategy targets is obstructed by severe, emerging threats. Biological evolution of the parasite and the mosquito vector, compounded by environmental instability, are eroding the efficacy of our most reliable medical interventions.

💊

Antimalarial Drug Resistance

The primary theme of the 2025 report. There is alarming evidence of Plasmodium falciparum mutations reducing the efficacy of Artemisinin-based Combination Therapies (ACTs). Originally confined to the Greater Mekong Subregion, these mutations are now appearing in parts of East and West Africa.

🌪️

Climate Change & Extreme Weather

Extreme weather events disrupt habitats and human populations. Severe flooding in Pakistan and cyclones in Southeast Africa have directly caused massive surges in localized malaria cases by creating vast new breeding grounds for mosquitoes and displacing vulnerable communities.

🦟

Invasive Vector Species

The rapid spread of Anopheles stephensi into urban environments across Africa presents a dire challenge. Unlike rural vectors, this species thrives in cities and demonstrates high resistance to many commonly used insecticidal interventions.
